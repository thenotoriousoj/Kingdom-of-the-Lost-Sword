This is a passion project for a video game I've had in my mind for some time. The summary offers a brief overview of what to expect. 

--------------------------------------------
WORLD GENERATION
The gameplay will involve a procedural map put together by hex tiles. The map will consist of biomes that become progressively more dangerous the further from the start you travel.
Each hex will belong to a class. Plain, Lumber, Quarry, Forsaken, and Dungeon.

BUILDING MECHANICS
Plain, Lumber, and Quarry tiles determine what may be built on them. Forsaken tiles cannot be built on. Dungeon tiles lead to boss fights.
You can teleport to settlements you build, allowing for quick traversal. You build a settlement outpost, and expand your settlement by building on adjacent tiles.
The outpost must be the first building of a settlement. A settlement has certain material and food requirements in order to sustain growth.
Food buildings are typically built on Plain tile classes. Material buildings are typically built on Quarry or Lumber tile classes.
Building a trader outpost in your settlement will allow you to trade with nearby settlements of other factions.

FACTION MECHANICS
There are 3 factions. The Faithful, The Horde, and The Forsaken. Each Race belongs to a specific faction.
The Faithful inherit Dwarves, Elves, Fairies, and similar races.
The Horde inherit Orcs, Ogres, Goblins, Trolls, and similar races.
The Forsaken inherit Skeletons, Vampires, Shades, Demons, Ghouls and similar races.
You will encounter friendly/neutral variants of the factions in their settlements, and aggressive variants in the wild.
Your reputation with each faction decrease when attacking members of their faction, whether instigated or not.
Your reputation will decrease also when increasing reputation with other factions.
Good reputation with The Forsaken decreases your reputation with both the Horde and the Faithful as they both are not fond of the Forsaken, and visa versa.
Good reputation allows better pricing when trading with that faction, more likely to encounter friendly variants in the wild, etc.
Bad reputation causes poorer pricing when trading, and that faction owns a settlement nearby a settlement of yours, they may raid your settlement.

COMBAT MECHANICS
The combat will revolve around your sword. Nearly every enemy has a chance of dropping a sword, with chances of rarity.
Your character will get strongther through experience, and through upgrades obtained through certain settlement buildings.
I am considering a skill tree.
The actual combat will be simple but have depth. Specifics will come in video.

THE LORE
Humans have nearly gone extinct in this otherworld.
Your people haven't crossed paths with this branch of humanity since the worlds separated at the time of the great awakening, when the grand wizard tore reality into two/
The grand wizard separated humanity from all other races to bring a safer and prosperous existence, yet some of humanity were forced through the veil into the otherworld.
After a distress signal was sent by some of the remaining magicians of the otherworld, you were chosen to reestablish the human race among that world as both a diplomat and a conqueror.
You are the distant heir of the first King of this world. As the offspring of the first Kings second son who was sent into the otherworld at the great awakening, you lived a life
not as prince or king, but of nobility, and when the King chose who to send to otherworld, you were seen as the rightful heir of the human kingdom in that domain.
However at the time of the start of your mission, you are only a knight in training, a Paige.
Essential to your mission is to recover the Lost Sword Aragmuthir of the late king Aragon, your distant father. 
The sword is rumored to hold great power, as well as the secret to humanities near extinction in that realm

X = Active Development Phase of the game. 0.2 = Player Character phase, 0.7 Sound Design
Y = Latest Update Number of Current Phase. 0.2.1 is first update of Phase 2, 0.3.4 is 4th update to phase 3.
Z = Predominant Changes made in this version.
0.1.2.7 would indicate this is the 2nd update during the world mapping phase of development, but that this specific update was predominantly sound design changes.
